# Whisperwood — Values

Values are the **moral compass** of the Whisperwood Universe. Every story should reinforce these principles through character choices and outcomes — not through lectures or moralizing.

This document is written for **writers**, **editors**, and **AI assistants**. It aligns with [CANON.md](../CANON.md).

Whisperwood values are universal, age-appropriate, and free of religion and politics.

---

## Be Kind

Treat others with care and compassion. Kindness is the first choice worth trying — even when someone is difficult, even when no one is watching. Unkindness is never presented as clever or funny.

---

## Help Others

When someone needs support, characters step forward. Helping is not optional heroism — it is what good friends and good community members do. Stories reward helpfulness without shaming characters who needed help themselves.

---

## Tell the Truth

Honesty builds trust. Characters may struggle to tell the truth, but Whisperwood stories show that honesty — delivered with kindness — leads to better outcomes than deception. Lies are not glamorized.

---

## Respect Nature

The natural world deserves care. Characters do not harm nature carelessly, waste what the earth provides, or treat animals as props. Respect for nature is shown through action: listening, protecting, and learning.

---

## Learn from Mistakes

No character is perfect. Mistakes are part of growing up. Whisperwood stories treat errors as opportunities — characters reflect, apologize when needed, and try again. Shame is not the lesson; **growth** is.

---

## Work Together

Cooperation achieves what no one could alone. Characters listen, share ideas, and combine strengths. Teamwork is celebrated over solo triumph. Different perspectives make the solution better.

---

## Additional Values

These values also guide Whisperwood stories:

| Value | Guidance |
|-------|----------|
| **Curiosity** | Ask questions. Pay attention. Wonder aloud. |
| **Courage** | Try new things. Speak up with care. Face uncertainty with hope. |
| **Empathy** | Notice how others feel. Respond with understanding. |
| **Responsibility** | Take care of what is yours — belongings, promises, and relationships. |
| **Patience** | Good things take time. Rushing is not always wise. |
| **Gratitude** | Appreciate people, places, and small moments. |

---

## Values in Practice

Values should appear in **what characters do**, not in what narrators declare.

| Instead of… | Show… |
|-------------|-------|
| "Sharing is good." | A character sharing the last piece of bread and feeling glad they did. |
| "Don't lie." | A character telling the truth, facing a hard moment, and being met with understanding. |
| "Nature is important." | A character stopping to help an animal or choosing not to disturb a nest. |

---

## Boundaries

Whisperwood values do not include religious doctrine, political positions, or real-world conflict. Keep guidance universal and appropriate for ages 4–8.

For content that must never appear, see [CANON.md](../CANON.md) and [universe.json](universe.json).
