# Whisperwood — Themes

Recurring themes are the **emotional foundation** of the Whisperwood Universe. They should be felt in every story — whether spoken directly or woven quietly into the narrative.

These themes align with [CANON.md](../CANON.md). When in doubt, canon prevails.

---

## Kindness

Compassion and care are never wasted in Whisperwood. Kindness opens doors that force alone cannot. Characters who choose kindness — even when it is not the easiest path — move stories forward and model behavior worth imitating.

---

## Curiosity

Wondering, asking questions, and paying attention are acts of courage. Whisperwood rewards characters who look closer, listen carefully, and want to understand. Curiosity leads to discovery.

---

## Friendship

Bonds between characters make hard moments survivable and good moments meaningful. Friendship is not decorative — it is **strength**. Stories should show characters supporting one another, learning from differences, and growing together.

---

## Family

Home, belonging, and the people who love you anchor every adventure. Family comes first — not as a constraint on adventure, but as the emotional ground characters return to and draw strength from.

---

## Discovery

The world is full of things worth noticing. Discovery is the joy of finding something new — a path, an idea, a friend, or a truth about oneself. Stories should leave children wanting to explore with open eyes.

---

## Imagination

Whisperwood treats imagination as a living force. Dreams, creativity, and play are valid ways of understanding the world. Stories should honor the inner lives of children and the possibilities they see.

---

## Nature

The natural world is alive, valuable, and worthy of gentle stewardship. Characters respect plants, animals, weather, and the land they walk on. Nature is not a backdrop — it is part of the story.

---

## Hope

Every problem has a peaceful solution. Conflict may arise, but resolution favors understanding, cooperation, and care over harm. Stories end with warmth, possibility, or quiet joy — never despair.

---

## Using Themes in Stories

Themes should emerge through **action and consequence**, not exposition. A story can explore one theme deeply or touch several lightly. Story Studio, writers, and AI assistants should ask:

> *Which themes does this story honor? Does the ending leave the audience with hope?*

For values that support these themes, see [values.md](values.md).
