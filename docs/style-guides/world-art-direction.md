# Whisperwood World Art Direction

> **Authority:** This document is the **highest authority for environmental artwork** in the Whisperwood Universe. When background, landscape, architecture, or atmosphere guidance conflicts with any other visual note, prompt, or production brief — except [CANON.md](../../CANON.md) — **this guide prevails.**

> **Scope:** All environments — natural, built, and interior — across books, animation, games, apps, YouTube, merchandise, marketing, and AI-generated media.

> **Companion document:** [Character Design Guide](character-design-guide.md) — highest authority for characters. Characters and environments must harmonize; neither overrides the other's domain.

---

## Purpose

Whisperwood is a **place** before it is a plot. Children do not only remember who they met — they remember how the forest light felt, whether the village seemed welcoming, and if the world itself felt safe enough to explore.

Environment consistency is essential because:

- **Trust is spatial.** A young audience decides whether to enter a story partly through setting. Inconsistent or frightening environments break trust — even when characters remain friendly.
- **The world is a character.** Whisperwood's forests, paths, and homes carry emotional meaning. They should feel as intentional as any protagonist.
- **Production multiplies.** Background artists, illustrators, game environment artists, and AI systems will each interpret "a cozy forest" differently unless a shared visual language exists.
- **Scale demands discipline.** Hundreds of locations across books, episodes, levels, and generated assets will drift without a single environmental authority.
- **Merchandise and brand extend from place.** Map puzzles, playsets, book endpapers, and app backgrounds all sell the **feeling of Whisperwood** — that feeling must be designed, not accidental.

This guide defines the **visual language of the world itself** — not individual location canon (see [locations/](../../locations/)) but the rules every Whisperwood environment must follow to belong.

## Overall Visual Philosophy

Whisperwood environments should feel like **stepping into a story you already trust** — a world that existed before the episode began and will remain gentle after it ends.

### The world should feel

| Quality | Environmental expression |
|---------|--------------------------|
| **Warm** | Golden and honey light, warm wood tones, hearth glow, sun-washed paths |
| **Peaceful** | Calm compositions, unhurried spaces, no visual chaos or aggressive geometry |
| **Cozy** | Human-scale nooks, soft edges, sheltered corners, inviting thresholds |
| **Safe** | Clear sight lines, approachable paths, no hidden threat staging |
| **Wonder-filled** | Layers of discovery — light through leaves, paths that bend, small details that reward looking |
| **Timeless** | No era-specific technology, fashion architecture, or trend-driven décor |
| **Nature-first** | The land leads; buildings nestle into terrain; paths follow the world rather than dominate it |

Nature is not wallpaper. It is the **primary author** of mood. Built spaces submit to trees, hills, water, and sky.

### Avoid

| Avoid | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| **Dystopian** | Broken infrastructure, ash skies, abandonment without warmth — violates hope |
| **Futuristic** | Sleek surfaces, neon cities, sci-fi machinery — breaks timeless nature-first identity |
| **Industrial** | Factories, pipelines, heavy metal architecture, pollution — cold and adult |
| **Dark fantasy** | Sharp spires, cursed forests, ominous ruins, skull motifs — frightens young audience |
| **Horror** | Fog as dread, dead trees as threat, caves as jaws — CANON exclusion |
| **Hyper realism** | Photographic texture, gritty grime, documentary gloom — wrong medium; Whisperwood is storybook |

When a creative choice sits near a boundary, choose the **warmer, softer, safer** environment. A child should want to **walk into** the scene, not shrink from it.

## Environment Style

Whisperwood environments share a **soft storybook landscape tradition** — painterly or hand-drawn in feel, readable in depth, rich in mid-ground detail, gentle in contrast. Backgrounds support characters; they never compete for attention.

Global rules for all environment types:

- **Layered depth:** foreground soft detail → clear mid-ground story space → simplified far distance
- **Curved horizons and paths** — rigid grid streets and ruler-straight vanishing points are rare
- **Atmospheric perspective** — distant hills soften and cool slightly; never murky smog
- **Character clearance:** story spaces leave readable ground for character placement and animation
- **Palette harmony:** environments use the world palette (see [Seasonal Palette](#seasonal-palette)); characters sit clearly against backgrounds

### Forests

The signature Whisperwood environment — where adventure begins and wonder lives.

- **Tree forms:** Rounded canopies, varied trunk bends, grouped masses — not uniform soldier rows
- **Understory:** Ferns, soft shrubs, mushrooms (friendly scale), fallen logs as seating — not obstacle courses
- **Forest floor:** Leaf litter, moss, dappled light patches — readable paths through
- **Depth:** Vertical layers — canopy, trunk mid-layer, ground — create discovery without clutter
- **Mood:** Inviting mystery — paths visible enough to follow; darkness is **shade**, not threat

Forests may be dense but never **entangling in a frightening way**. Always a sense that home or help is reachable.

### Villages

Human community at child scale — places where neighbors know each other.

- **Scale:** Low buildings, short lanes, visible sky — no canyon streets
- **Layout:** Organic clustering around a green, well, or gathering tree — not rigid grids
- **Signs of life:** Laundry lines, flower boxes, stacked firewood, bicycles or carts — gentle activity
- **Edges:** Village transitions softly into meadow or forest — not walled fortress
- **Mood:** Belonging, safety, gentle bustle

### Rivers

Moving water as guide and companion.

- **Flow:** Curving course, visible current lines, gentle rapids at most — no whitewater peril
- **Banks:** Soft mud, stones, reeds, overhanging branches — places to sit and wonder
- **Bridges:** Wood or stone, arched or flat, hand-crafted — never massive steel
- **Sound implied visually:** Sparkle on riffles, smooth pools — calm movement
- **Mood:** Journey, freshness, life — water carries stories forward

### Lakes

Still water as mirror and rest.

- **Surface:** Reflective but readable — sky and tree reflection simplified, not photoreal glass
- **Shores:** Gradual entry — beaches of small stones, grassy banks, dock or flat rock
- **Depth cues:** Color shift toward center — never black abyss implication
- **Mood:** Pause, reflection, shared quiet — emotional breathing room in stories

### Mountains

Elevation without intimidation.

- **Forms:** Rounded peaks and soft ridges — not jagged alpine knives
- **Snow line:** Gentle cap, if present — not avalanche terrain
- **Paths:** Visible switchbacks at readable scale — achievement without vertigo
- **Foreground:** Wildflowers, goats or birds optional — life at every elevation
- **Mood:** Achievement, vista, fresh air — awe without fear

### Meadows

Open joy and social gathering.

- **Grass:** Tall enough for texture, short enough for visibility — waving seed heads, wildflowers
- **Sky dominance:** More open sky than forest — brightness and air
- **Features:** Lone tree, stone, path crossing, picnic possibility
- **Mood:** Freedom, play, conversation, discovery at ground level (insects, flowers)

### Roads

Paths that invite walking.

- **Materials:** Packed earth, gravel, worn stone — not asphalt highways
- **Width:** Child-scale — wide enough for two friends side by side, not vehicle lanes
- **Edges:** Defined by grass, flowers, or low stone — not curbs and traffic paint
- **Marking:** No lane lines, no billboards, no signage clutter
- **Mood:** Forward motion, choice at forks, return routes visible

### Homes

Shelter as emotional anchor — **family comes first** in CANON.

- **Exterior:** Pitched roof, chimney with gentle smoke optional, porch or step, garden proximity
- **Materials:** Wood, stone, plaster — warm surfaces, never cold glass towers
- **Scale:** Cottage and farmhouse logic — rooms implied, not mansion sprawl
- **Threshold:** Door as invitation — visible, lit, approachable
- **Mood:** Return, safety, belonging, warmth waiting inside

### Interiors

Indoor spaces extend the cozy world — never sterile or minimalist cold.

- **Rooms:** Living space, kitchen, bedroom — readable furniture, human scale
- **Light:** Window light primary; lamp and hearth secondary — warm pools, soft shadows
- **Clutter (gentle):** Books, mugs, quilts, drawings on wall — signs of life, not mess
- **Ceiling:** Visible beams welcome; low enough to feel enclosed safely
- **Mood:** Hearth, rest, family, preparation for tomorrow's adventure

## Architecture

Whisperwood buildings are **hand-made and human-scaled** — they look as if someone who cares built them over time, with local materials and rounded patience.

### Core principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Simple** | Clear massing — one or two roof volumes; additions feel organic, not chaotic |
| **Wood** | Timber frames, shingles, plank walls — warm grain, never factory perfect |
| **Stone** | Foundations, chimneys, low walls — rounded river stone or soft-cut blocks |
| **Natural materials** | Clay tile, thatch, plaster, rope, iron hardware — honest and tactile |
| **Rounded shapes** | Arched doorways, circular windows, bowed roofs — avoid sharp brutalism |
| **Warm windows** | Glow from within at evening — invitation, not surveillance |
| **Hand-crafted feeling** | Slight asymmetry, visible craft, local character |

### Building types

- **Homes:** Cottages, farmhouses, tree-adjacent cabins
- **Community:** Small schoolhouse, village hall, market stall, library nook
- **Workshops:** Carpenter, baker, fix-it shed — one-room clarity
- **Bridges and gates:** Transition architecture — marks journey without blocking

### Never include

- Modern suburbs — tract housing, garages dominant, driveway culture
- Skyscrapers — any high-rise cityscape
- Glass-and-steel towers, parking structures, industrial warehouses
- Brutalist concrete, chain-store façades, billboards
- Military fortification aesthetic — walls exist to define cozy space, not war

Technology in Whisperwood is **simple and child-friendly** ([CANON.md](../../CANON.md)). Architecture reflects hand tools, not digital age skyline.

## Nature

Natural elements are the **primary visual vocabulary** of Whisperwood. They should feel alive, welcoming, and worth respecting.

### Trees

- Varied species **silhouette** — oak-like spread, birch-like slenderness, pine-like soft cones — stylized, not botanical plate
- Trunks lean slightly — personality, not hurricane damage
- Canopies as **soft cloud shapes** — layered, translucent to light
- Seasonal honesty — spring bud, summer full, autumn gold, winter bare but gentle

### Flowers

- Wildflower scatter — dots of color in grass, not formal estate beds exclusively
- Readable simple shapes — daisy, bell, cup — identifiable at storybook scale
- Color accents — never neon; harmonize with season palette

### Grass

- Stroke or blade texture suggested — not individual blade photorealism
- Movement implied — bend direction in wind, flatten on paths
- Color variation — yellow-green highlights, deeper green shade — not uniform carpet

### Rocks

- Rounded river stones, soft boulders, flat stepping rocks
- Moss and lichen welcome — age without decay
- Never sharp obsidian shards, cliff faces as fangs, or unstable rockfall staging

### Clouds

- Cumulus logic — piled, soft, readable shapes
- Sky gradient warm toward horizon
- Storm clouds soft-edged — dramatic at most, never tornado or hurricane visual

### Stars

- Simple points or soft crosses — not dense milky-way realism
- Constellations suggested playfully — connect-the-dots wonder, not astrophysics chart
- Night sky deep blue or soft violet — not void black

### Sunlight

- Primary mood engine — dappled through leaves, long afternoon rays, morning freshness
- Lens flare and bloom **minimal** — warmth, not spectacle
- Shadows soft-edged — never harsh noir contrast in children's story space

### Rain

- Visible drops or streaks — silver slant, puddle circles
- Characters may get wet — never cold misery aesthetic
- Sky gray-blue warm base — not green-black storm terror

### Fog

- **Milk-soft layers** — depth simplification, mystery without hidden threat
- Shapes emerge gently — tree crowns, roof peaks — not figures in mist
- Always resolves — fog lifts, path continues visible

### Snow

- Soft accumulation on rounded surfaces — caps on posts, branch pillows
- Footprints and sled tracks — play evidence
- Sky bright or softly overcast — winter cozy, not blizzard survival

**Everything in nature should feel welcoming.** Even weather that changes plans remains a world a child would want to visit.

## Lighting

Light carries emotion. Whisperwood lighting is **story-first** — it tells the audience how to feel before dialogue begins.

### Time-of-day lighting

| Time | Character | Story use |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Golden hour** | Honey light, long shadows, warm rim on characters | Default hero light — adventure, friendship, arrival home |
| **Morning** | Fresh, cool-warm mix, dew brightness, crisp sky | New beginnings, discovery departures |
| **Afternoon** | Even, clear, active — full color saturation | Play, journey midpoints, outdoor activity |
| **Evening** | Softening sun, lengthening shadows, first warm window glow | Winding down, reflection, homeward travel |
| **Night** | Deep blue-violet sky, not pure black | Gentle night adventure — still safe |
| **Moonlight** | Cool silver wash, soft contrast, readable silhouettes | Quiet wonder, whisper conversations — never horror blue |
| **Lantern light** | Warm orange pool, falloff into soft dark | Guidance, gathering, cozy mystery |
| **Indoor lighting** | Hearth, lamp, window spill — warm dominant | Safety, family, rest |

### Lighting principles

- **Characters remain readable** — rim or key light separates them from background
- **Shadows are soft** — no horror chiaroscuro in standard story spaces
- **Motivated sources** — sun, moon, lamp, fire — avoid arbitrary rainbow lighting
- **Emotional alignment** — worry scenes may dim slightly; resolution returns warmth
- **Magic light** — gentle glow only ([CANON.md](../../CANON.md)); never lightning-storm spectacle as default

Lighting should **support emotional storytelling**, not decorate it. If the light says "danger" but the story says "cozy," the light is wrong.

## Seasonal Palette

Each season has a **color story** and **emotional mood**. Environments shift seasonally; identity remains Whisperwood.

### Spring

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| **Colors** | Fresh green, blossom pink and white, pale yellow, rain-washed sky blue |
| **Mood** | Renewal, curiosity, first steps outdoors |
| **Nature** | Buds, blossoms, new grass, returning birds implied |
| **Light** | Clear morning, soft rain sparkle |

### Summer

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| **Colors** | Full green, golden yellow, sky blue, flower accents, warm skin-tone highlights |
| **Mood** | Play, adventure, fullness, long-day joy |
| **Nature** | Dense canopy, meadows in bloom, lazy river |
| **Light** | Bright afternoon, golden hour adventure |

### Autumn

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| **Colors** | Amber, rust orange, honey gold, moss green, warm brown, soft purple dusk |
| **Mood** | Gathering, reflection, cozy preparation, gentle change |
| **Nature** | Falling leaves, seed heads, harvest tables, misty mornings |
| **Light** | Golden hour dominant — Whisperwood autumn is especially warm |

### Winter

| Element | Direction |
|---------|-----------|
| **Colors** | Soft white, blue-gray shadow, warm window orange, evergreen, bark brown |
| **Mood** | Rest, warmth indoors, quiet wonder, togetherness |
| **Nature** | Snow softness, bare branches delicate not dead, icicles decorative |
| **Light** | Bright snow bounce or cozy interior contrast — never bleak gray-only worlds |

Seasonal records for canonical locations may be stored in `location.profile.json` → `seasonality`. This guide defines the **palette rules** those records must follow.

## Weather

Weather creates **atmosphere and story opportunity** — never terror. Children experience weather as mood, not threat.

| Weather | Whisperwood treatment |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Rain** | Cozy — puddles for jumping, umbrellas, porch watching, steam from tea — sound implied as comfort |
| **Fog** | Mysterious but safe — hidden path reveals, soft shapes, gentle surprise — always lifts |
| **Wind** | Playful — leaves chase, scarves flutter, hats saved by friends — not destruction |
| **Snow** | Soft play — packing, tracks, sledding gentle slopes — cold paired with warmth indoors |
| **Sun** | Default joy — shade available when needed under trees |
| **Cloud cover** | Soft even light — calm or thoughtful scenes — not prelude to horror storm |
| **Storm (if used)** | Distant rumble, brief pass, shelter together — no lightning on characters, no flooded peril |

Weather may **delay, redirect, or cozy-up** a story. It must not **punish or frighten** the audience. After weather passes, warmth returns.

## Camera Philosophy

How the world is framed is as important as what it contains. Whisperwood favors **child-height honesty** and **environmental storytelling**.

### Wide shots

- Establish place — forest edge, village green, lake shore
- Show relationship between characters and world — small in cozy vastness, not lost in emptiness
- Horizon placement mid or lower — sky generous, ground inviting
- Use for: departures, arrivals, wonder beats, season establishing

### Close shots

- Detail storytelling — flower, footprint, door handle, steam from cup
- Texture of wood grain, moss, fabric — tactile warmth
- Use for: discovery clues, gentle mystery, emotional pause

### Eye level

- Default camera height matches **child protagonist** — not aerial drone default, not adult chest height only
- Adults and mentors may look taller from this angle — natural, not imposing
- World feels experienced **with** characters, not observed from above as surveillance

### Child perspective

- Doorways loom gently large — threshold as event
- Paths extend forward — invitation down the frame
- Objects of interest at reachable height — not museum display distance
- Wonder comes from **looking up and looking close** — canopy height, ground detail

### Environmental storytelling

Backgrounds tell story without words:

- Worn path = many journeys
- Open gate = welcome
- Lit window = someone home
- Two cups on table = company expected
- Footprints in snow = friend went this way

Every establishing shot should answer: **What happened here before the scene?** and **Is it safe to enter?**

Structured per-location camera notes may live in `location.profile.json` → `cameraProfile`. This guide defines the **philosophy** those profiles extend.

## AI Environment Generation

AI systems will generate backgrounds, landscapes, buildings, and weather at scale. Without rules, each output drifts toward generic fantasy, photorealism, or dark cinematic defaults.

### Authority order for AI environmental work

1. [CANON.md](../../CANON.md) — world rules, safety, content boundaries
2. **This guide** — environmental visual language
3. [Character Design Guide](character-design-guide.md) — harmony with character style when both appear
4. [locations/{id}/profile.json](../../locations/) — location-specific facts, atmosphere, profiles
5. [story-frameworks/](../../story-frameworks/) — staging intent for story type (optional context)

### Generate without changing artistic identity

| Asset type | Consistency anchors |
|------------|---------------------|
| **Backgrounds** | Soft storybook depth layers, warm palette, nature-first, no photoreal grit |
| **Landscapes** | Rounded terrain, readable paths, welcoming sky, season-appropriate palette |
| **Buildings** | Wood/stone, hand-crafted, rounded, warm windows — never modern city |
| **Roads** | Earth paths, child scale, no asphalt highways |
| **Trees** | Soft canopy masses, varied silhouettes, dappled light |
| **Sky** | Gradient warmth, friendly clouds, stars simple — moon soft |
| **Weather** | Cozy rain, safe fog, playful wind — atmosphere not terror |

### Using location `imageProfile`

When canonical locations define `imageProfile` or `promptProfile`:

- Merge location prompts **with** this guide — never override safety or style rules
- `negativePrompt` should include: dystopian, futuristic, industrial, horror, photorealistic, modern city, skyscraper, dark fantasy, harsh neon, storm terror
- `artStyle`, `lighting`, `renderingStyle`, `cameraStyle` must harmonize with this document

**Do not reference specific AI models, engines, or proprietary settings** in canonical records. Describe visual outcomes only.

### AI assistant checklist (before generating environments)

- [ ] Read this guide and relevant location JSON if assigned
- [ ] Apply nature-first, warm, cozy, safe, wonder-filled philosophy
- [ ] Use storybook painterly style — not hyper realism
- [ ] Match season and time-of-day palette
- [ ] Frame at child eye level unless wide establishing requires otherwise
- [ ] Leave readable space for characters
- [ ] Exclude: horror, dystopia, industrial, dark fantasy, modern suburbs, skyscrapers
- [ ] Validate: Would a child aged 4–8 want to step into this scene?

### Video and animation environments

- **Parallax layers** preserve depth language — foreground, mid, far
- **Moving elements** — grass sway, cloud drift, rain — soft and loop-friendly
- **Lighting continuity** — sun angle consistent across shot unless time passes in story
- **No chaotic detail** that flickers or moirés at compression (YouTube, mobile)

## Long-term Scalability

Whisperwood will grow across **books, animation, games, YouTube, merchandise, interactive apps, and future AI tools**. World art direction is the **spatial immune system** — one visual language, many outputs.

### Format support

| Format | How this guide helps |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Books** | Consistent endpaper maps, chapter headers, full-spread environments — same forest everywhere |
| **Animation** | Background model sheets, color scripts, lighting keys — pipeline onboarding document |
| **Games** | Tileable terrain rules, biome palette limits, readable collision spaces — art bible for level artists |
| **YouTube** | Thumbnail-safe wide shots, compression-friendly simplicity, warm identity at small size |
| **Merchandise** | Playmat forests, puzzle skies, playset cottages — product line reads one world |
| **Interactive apps** | Parallax layer rules, seasonal variants, safe night modes — reusable environment grammar |
| **Future AI tools** | Outcome-based rules survive model changes better than tool-specific prompts |

### Location onboarding pipeline

When creating a new canonical location:

1. Design environment against this guide — not against a single reference image mood board alone
2. Validate architecture, nature, lighting, and season palette
3. Record structured data in `location.profile.json` → `geography`, `atmosphere`, `seasonality`, `imageProfile`, `cameraProfile`, `weatherProfile`
4. Write expanded notes in location Markdown profile when present
5. Submit for editorial review before `status: canonical`

### Harmony with characters

Environments and characters share **soft storybook warmth** but different domains:

- Characters: highest authority → [Character Design Guide](character-design-guide.md)
- Environments: highest authority → **this document**
- When character and background styles conflict, **both guides apply** — adjust until harmony; escalate if impossible

### Revision policy

Changes to this guide affect **every location, every background plate, every generated landscape**. Revisions require intentional authorship — same standard as [CANON.md](../../CANON.md).

Whisperwood today should **look like Whisperwood** in ten years — the same forest light, the same village welcome.

### Success metric

> A child who closes their eyes after a Whisperwood story should still feel the warmth of the path, the glow of the window, and the urge to look closer at the trees.

That is the standard this guide exists to protect.
