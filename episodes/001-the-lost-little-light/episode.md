## Overview

One morning, while exploring together on a familiar path at the edge of Whisperwood, **Zulk** and **Zaya** notice something unexpected — a **tiny glowing light** drifting gently between the trees.

It is not scary. It is not loud. It is simply **there** — soft, small, and slowly moving as if it does not know where to go.

The siblings are curious. They wonder. And when the light begins to fade and wander, they realize it might be **lost**.

Instead of keeping it, they choose to **help it find its way home**.

Episode 001 is intentionally simple: one problem, two protagonists, six scenes, and a warm ending that opens the door to many more adventures. It proves the flagship duo can carry a story alone — Zulk with forward curiosity, Zaya with gentle empathy — without villains, danger, or magic that solves everything for them.

**Target audience:** Ages 4–8  
**Estimated duration:** 6–8 minutes

---

## Learning Goal

**Helping others without expecting a reward.**

Zulk and Zaya do not capture the light, keep it as a prize, or ask for something in return. They help because help is needed — and the story ends with warmth, not treasure.

The lesson is shown through action: slowing down, listening, walking beside something small and uncertain, and feeling glad when it finds belonging.

## Beginning

A peaceful **morning**. Zulk and Zaya walk together on a path they know — siblings side by side, explorer backpack and magic lantern present.

Zulk pauses. He points.

Between the trees, a **tiny light** floats — no bigger than a fingertip, glowing softly gold.

Zaya's eyes widen. She steps closer, slowly.

**Curiosity** opens the story. No chase. No alarm. Just: *what is that?*

---

## Middle

The light drifts onward. The siblings follow at a walking pace — not running.

For a moment, it seems like a game. **Wonder** brightens their faces.

Then the light **flickers** and dims. It circles back, then stops — as if unsure.

Zaya tilts her head. She feels something the way she often does: the light is not playing. It is **lost**.

Zulk wants to go faster — to lead it toward the path home. But rushing makes the light pull away.

**Concern** settles in — quiet, not scary.

They try together. Zulk watches where it moves. Zaya suggests they **wait and listen**. Birdsong, breeze, the smell of wet moss — and ahead, a brighter patch of morning sun.

**Cooperation** replaces hurry.

---

## Ending

In a **sunlit meadow**, other tiny glows rest among the grass and flowers — steady, calm, at home.

The lost little light brightens. It drifts forward and settles beside them, glowing warmly again.

Zulk and Zaya do not touch it. They smile.

No reward. No prize. Just the feeling of **hope** fulfilled — and then **joy**, quiet and shared.

They look at each other, then down the path.

The forest is still full of morning. Whatever adventure comes next can wait a moment.

The episode closes on the siblings walking on — **ready to wonder again**.

---

## Lesson

Helping someone find their way — **patiently, kindly, and without expecting anything back** — is worth doing.

The audience should feel:

- Curiosity is the first step toward caring
- Pausing can be braver than rushing
- Brothers and sisters can help best when they combine different strengths
- A happy ending does not require keeping what you found

No character states this as a moral. The meadow and the smiles say enough.

## Creative Notes

### Do

- Keep stakes gentle and readable for ages 4–8
- Let Zulk and Zaya both contribute to resolution
- Use child eye-level framing per style guides
- End with hope and forward-looking wonder
- Honor [CANON.md](../../CANON.md) — kindness, curiosity, peaceful resolution

### Do not

- Add villains, chases, combat, or genuine peril
- Let magic lantern or any magic **solve** the problem
- Introduce new recurring characters or named places without approval
- Preach the learning goal in dialogue
- Frighten with dark forests or aggressive staging
