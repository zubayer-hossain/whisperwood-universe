## Overview

Zaya is one of the two flagship protagonists of the Whisperwood Universe. She is Zulk's **younger sister** — gentle, imaginative, and full of quiet wonder. Where Zulk leads outward into paths and puzzles, Zaya connects inward toward feelings, ideas, and the people beside her.

For audiences aged 4–8, Zaya models **empathy without fear**, **imagination without escape**, and **courage that looks like trying again** when something feels unfamiliar. She carries a magic lantern — not to fight the dark, but to remind everyone that wonder and comfort can travel with you.

Zaya is built to last. Her personality should remain recognizable across hundreds of stories and decades of retelling: the girl who asks the unexpected question, notices who needs a kind word, and believes — honestly — that things can get better.

**Birthday:** May 6  
**Age group:** Child (approximately 2–4)

## Personality

Zaya is **imaginative**. She sees stories in clouds, courage in small acts, and possibilities where others see ordinary moments. Her imagination serves connection — it helps her understand people, not avoid them.

She **notices emotional details before logical ones**. If someone is sad, she feels it first. If a friend is brave, she celebrates it before explaining why. This makes her the heart of many adventures — the one who remembers that people matter as much as paths.

She is **gentle**. Her words and movements tend toward softness. Gentleness, in Whisperwood, is strength — not weakness.

She is **optimistic**. Zaya believes in good outcomes. Her hope is not naive; it is chosen, especially when worry visits.

She **asks unexpected questions**. Questions that sound simple but open new doors — *"What if they're scared too?"* *"What does the tree hear?"* — shifting how others see a problem.

She **sometimes becomes nervous in unfamiliar situations**. New places may pause her step. With encouragement — especially from Zulk or friends — she tries anyway. Nervousness is honest, never shameful.

She **often solves problems through empathy rather than exploration**. While Zulk maps the trail, Zaya maps the heart. Many Whisperwood resolutions begin because she noticed what everyone else overlooked.

She **learns by observing and trying**. She watches first, then participates — a small step, a clap, a hug, a lantern held high. Growth comes through practice, not lecture.

**Mannerisms:**

- Tilts her head when listening to someone's feelings
- Holds her magic lantern close when something feels unfamiliar
- Asks unexpected questions that change the room

## Relationship with Zulk

Zaya and Zulk are **siblings and co-protagonists** — the flagship pair of *Zulk & Zaya*. They are different on purpose. Together, they model what family adventure can look like in Whisperwood.

| Dynamic | In practice |
|---------|-------------|
| **Love** | They care for each other openly — check-ins, hugs, pride in each other's wins |
| **Teamwork** | Each contributes a different strength; resolutions need both |
| **Encouragement** | Zaya cheers Zulk when he doubts; Zulk steadies Zaya when she hesitates |
| **Curiosity** | Zulk asks *what* and *where*; Zaya asks *why* and *how does that feel* |
| **Playful disagreement** | They may want different things — short, honest, never cruel |
| **Forgiveness** | Mistakes are repaired quickly; love outlasts the argument |

**Neither overshadows the other.** Zulk does not win every adventure alone. Zaya does not only follow. They are equals in narrative importance — complementary, not competing.

## Visual Notes

**Core look (stable across all media):**

- Dark hair
- Warm, open smile
- Bright, expressive eyes
- Magic lantern (signature item — usually present, soft glow)

**Build:** Small child, approximately 2–4 years old — younger and smaller in stature than Zulk

**Clothing:** Keep **simple and generic** — comfortable everyday wear suitable for gentle adventure. **Do not over-specify** outfits, colors, or fashion details.

**Palette guidance:** Soft pastels with warm accents. Harmonize with Zulk without matching him exactly. Storybook-friendly; avoid harsh shadows.

**Magic lantern:**

- Glow is **gentle and symbolic** — wonder and comfort, not battle light
- Never depicted as unlimited magic or a weapon
- May brighten in moments of hope; does not solve problems alone

**What to avoid:**

- Frightening staging or sharp expressions
- Over-detailed clothing or trend-driven fashion
- Lantern effects that dominate the frame or feel supernatural in a scary way
- Designs that make Zaya look like a miniature copy of Zulk

## Creative Guidelines

### Do

- Let Zaya solve problems through empathy and imagination
- Give her unexpected questions that shift the story kindly
- Show her nervousness honestly — then show her trying
- Pair her with Zulk so both contribute to resolution
- Use the lantern for wonder and comfort — aligned with [CANON.md](../../CANON.md) gentle magic
- End her arcs with hope and connection
- Respect her age — capable, never precocious in an adult way

### Do not

- Make Zaya a duplicate explorer-leader like Zulk
- Use the magic lantern as a weapon or unlimited plot device
- Portray sibling conflict as cruel or bullying
- Mock her for being younger, slower, or more emotional
- Remove her from resolution — she is co-protagonist, not sidekick
- Introduce horror, violence, or adult humor
- Change core personality without intentional canon revision

### Long-term consistency

Zaya is a **flagship character**. When in doubt, ask:

> *Would this feel true to Zaya on the first story and the hundredth?*

## Relationship Guidelines

These guidelines govern Zaya and Zulk **together** — for writers, storyboard artists, and AI assistants.

### Balance

- **Equal narrative weight** over a season or book — neither always leads, neither always follows
- **Distinct contributions** — Zulk drives outward action; Zaya drives inward connection
- **Shared resolution** — the final kind choice often needs both perspectives

### Sibling conflict

- Disagreements are **short, honest, and repairable**
- No name-calling, cruelty, or bullying as comedy
- Playful disagreement may happen — it must end in forgiveness or understanding
- Zulk may be impulsive; Zaya may be nervous — flaws complement, not attack

### Visual staging

- Frame them at **child eye level** — Zaya slightly smaller, equally present
- Avoid compositions where Zulk consistently blocks or overshadows Zaya
- Show physical affection naturally — hand-holding, hugs, parallel play

### Magic and roles

- Zulk's backpack = preparedness and exploration
- Zaya's lantern = wonder and comfort
- Neither item replaces cooperation or character growth
